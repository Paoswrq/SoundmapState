How To Use

Soundmap is an interactive sound visualizer that responds in real time to both ambient sounds and direct input like your voice or keyboard. It features three unique visual modes—Waveform, Fireburst, and 3D Blocks—which automatically rotate every 10 seconds. Each mode offers a distinct vibe: Waveform draws smooth, flowing lines that trace the shape of the sound; Fireburst was originally meant to resemble an explosion, but I’ve come to like its bubbling, energetic feel even more; and 3D Blocks gives a sense of motion and depth, as if you’re flying through a tunnel of blocks—or they’re flying toward you.

The pitch of your input—whether spoken or from the keyboard—directly affects the color of the visuals. Higher pitches generate brighter hues, while lower pitches result in deeper tones. What's more, the background color is dynamically set to the inverse of the main color, creating a vibrant contrast that enhances the experience. The color will gradually fade to gray if no pitch is detected. Try pressing keys on the piano to see how each one lights up the screen with a different color, guided by the letter and color overlay on the visual.

In quiet environments, you can speak or make soft sounds (like tapping the laptop) to activate the visualizer. If a loud sound exceeds the volume threshold, the auto-switching timer resets—so you’ll have to wait 10 seconds from that moment for the next mode change. Enjoy exploring the sound and color of your surroundings with Soundmap!
